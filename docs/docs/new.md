Self-Correcting Proportional Recognition Properties

The system naturally promotes accurate proportional recognition (correct proportional recognition of goal prioritization and contributor contributions) through mathematical necessity:

Proportional Recognition Accuracy and Network Integrity:

Participants define their goals subjectively, but achieving them depends on objective access to resources and contributions. 

Proportional recognition accuracy is validated through outcomes:

True Recognition: Recognition of proportions that, when acted upon, connect you with beneficial/needed resources (validated by positive outcomes)

False Recognition: Recognition of proportions that fail to connect you with beneficial/needed resources (invalidated by negative outcomes)

Mathematical Consequence:

 For any participant:
 Total Recognition Budget = 100%
 Total Recognition = True Recognition + False Recognition
 
 Therefore:
 ↑ False Recognition → ↓ True Recognition
   → ↑ Capacity being allocated to non-beneficial partners → ↓ Capacity being allocated to beneficial partners
   → ↓ Probability of Goal Achievement
   → Natural incentive to correct priority distribution
             
Key Implication: The system creates natural incentives for accurate proportional recognition. Inflation or misattribution of priorities and only decreases connection to actually beneficial partners. Participants that maintain accurate recognition of proportions (goal prioritization, and contribution) better-align their capacity allocation and achieve better outcomes.
