All social outcomes arise from how finite human capacities are allocated across shared relations. When capacity flows toward relations that amplify it—through tools, knowledge, coordination, and accumulated systems—individual effort produces effects far beyond its immediate scale. Alignment exists not as a fixed arrangement, but as the ability of capacity to move: to shift toward relations that demonstrably expand what agents can realize together, and away from those that do not. Misalignment is not error in intention but friction in motion—where capacity is trapped, misdirected, or insulated from feedback. A society is free to the degree that it enables rapid discovery and redirection: that agents can see what works, withdraw from what fails, and reallocate their capacities without permission or penalty. The true measure of coordination is not how little effort is required, nor how perfectly plans are designed, but how quickly capacity can realign itself toward relations that multiply possibility. Alignment is velocity. Freedom is the speed at which misallocation disappears.

\section*{Social Capacity (Compressed)}

As production becomes increasingly social, outcomes depend less on isolated effort and more on how individual capacity is allocated into relations that amplify its effectiveness. Capacity does not disappear; its causal role is transformed from direct production to relational activation.

Allocation determines where capacity is directed, alignment determines whether that direction engages amplifying relations, and amplification determines the scale of outcomes. These are not separate processes but successive moments of a single causal chain.

\begin{equation}
O_i = \sum_{j} X_{ij} \cdot A_j
\end{equation}

Where: \\
$X_{ij}$ = capacity allocated by agent $i$ to relation or system $j$ \\
$A_j$ = amplification generated by relation $j$ \\
$O_i$ = outcome realized by agent $i$

In developed social systems, amplification grows faster than direct contribution. Outcomes therefore cease to be proportional to effort and become proportional to alignment: relative results track relative access to amplifying relations rather than relative input.

\begin{equation}
\frac{O_i}{O_k} \approx \frac{\sum_j X_{ij} A_j}{\sum_j X_{kj} A_j}
\end{equation}

Alignment holds when agents who benefit from amplifying relations retain viable pathways to reciprocally sustain them. Misalignment appears when capacity is structurally prevented from flowing back into the relations that generate outcomes.

\noindent\rule{\textwidth}{0.5pt}
\textbf{As the number of possible relations and amplifications grows, the space of viable allocations expands without bound. The horizon is not the end of necessity, but the indefinite expansion of how capacity may be aligned with the relations that magnify it.}
\rule{\textwidth}{0.5pt}

\section*{The Fundamental Connection (Amplification is Goal-Relative)}

\textbf{The Causal Chain:}
\[
\underset{\text{``What matters''}}{W_i} \xrightarrow{\quad} \underset{\text{``Where capacity goes''}}{X_{ij}} \xrightarrow{\quad} \underset{\text{``How much it multiplies''}}{A_j} \xrightarrow{\quad} \underset{\text{``What actually happens''}}{O_i}
\]

Amplification is not intrinsic; it is the projection of a system's effects onto an agent's values.

\begin{equation}
U_i = \sum_{j} X_{ij} \cdot A_j^{(i)}
\end{equation}

\subsection*{Derivation}
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Goals define success:} $U_i = \sum_k W_{ik} G_{ik}$
\item \textbf{Goals require outcomes:} $G_{ik} \approx \sum_m \beta_{km} O_{im}$
\item \textbf{Outcomes require allocation:} $O_{im} = \sum_j X_{ij} A_{jm}$
\end{enumerate}

Substituting back yields \textbf{Effective Amplification}:

\begin{equation}
A_j^{(i)} = \sum_k \sum_m W_{ik} \beta_{km} A_{jm}
\end{equation}

Where: \\
$W_{ik}$ = weight agent $i$ assigns to goal $k$ \\
$\beta_{km}$ = how outcome $m$ serves goal $k$ \\
$A_{jm}$ = objective output $m$ from system $j$

\textbf{Implication:} Two agents can allocate to the same system $j$ but experience opposite amplification $A_j^{(i)}$ if their goal weights $W_{ik}$ differ.

\section*{The Single Summary Equation}

This equation ties it all together—allocation constraints, goal-relative amplification, and sustainability:

\begin{equation}
O_i \text{ (or } U_i \text{)} = \sum_{j} X_{ij} \cdot A_j^{(i)} \quad \text{with} \quad X_{ij} \le C_i \;\; \land \;\; X_{ij} = 0 \;\; \text{if misaligned}
\end{equation}

\subsection*{Interpretation}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Allocation} $\rightarrow X_{ij}$: where capacity goes
\item \textbf{Constraint} $\rightarrow C_i$: total finite capacity of agent $i$
\item \textbf{Alignment} $\rightarrow$ whether $X_{ij}$ is viable at all
\item \textbf{Relative Amplification} $\rightarrow A_j^{(i)}$: how much that direction multiplies value for agent $i$
\item \textbf{Unsustainability} $\rightarrow$ losing access to nonzero $X_{ij}$
\item \textbf{Free riding} $\rightarrow$ extracting $A_j$ without maintaining conditions for $X_{ij} > 0$
\end{itemize}

\section*{Correction Velocity (Same Equation Family)}

We start from the core relation with time dynamics (where $O_i$ represents realized value/utility):

\begin{equation}
O_i(t) = \sum_j X_{ij}(t) A_j^{(i)}(t)
\end{equation}

\subsection*{1. Misalignment as gradient error}

Define misalignment as the gap between realized outcomes and attainable outcomes given available relations:

\begin{equation}
\Delta_i(t) = O_i^*(t) - O_i(t)
\end{equation}

Where: \\
$O_i^*(t)$ = achievable outcome under optimal alignment \\
$\Delta_i(t)$ = lost goal realization due to misallocation

\subsection*{2. Capacity updates as hypothesis revision}

Agents update allocations in response to observed amplification:

\begin{equation}
\frac{dX_{ij}}{dt} = \eta \left( A_j^{(i)} - \frac{1}{C_i} \sum_{j'} X_{ij'} A_{j'}^{(i)} \right)
\end{equation}

Where the second term ensures $\sum_j X_{ij} = C_i$ remains constant (capacity is conserved).

Since $\frac{\partial O_i}{\partial X_{ij}} = A_j^{(i)}$, allocation updates are driven by the difference between specific and component-average amplification.

\subsection*{3. Correction velocity (the key metric)}

Correction velocity is the rate at which misalignment decays:

\begin{equation}
V_i = -\frac{d\Delta_i}{dt}
\end{equation}

Or equivalently:

\begin{equation}
V_i \propto \frac{d}{dt}\left(\sum_j X_{ij} A_j^{(i)}\right)
\end{equation}

\textbf{Interpretation:} Correction velocity is how fast capacity is reallocated toward higher-amplification relations.

\subsection*{4. Infrastructure as acceleration, not direction}

Infrastructure conditions affect how fast updates occur, not where capacity must go:

\begin{equation}
V_i = \eta \cdot \sum_j |\Delta X_{ij}| \cdot A_j^{(i)}
\end{equation}

Where: \\
$|\Delta X_{ij}|$ = reallocatable capacity per unit time

High correction velocity requires:
\begin{itemize}
\item high $\eta$ (fast learning)
\item low switching cost (large $\Delta X$)
\item legible $A_j^{(i)}$
\end{itemize}

\section*{Unified Chain (Allocation $\rightarrow$ Alignment $\rightarrow$ Amplification $\rightarrow$ Velocity)}

The entire dynamic in one line:

\begin{equation}
\frac{d}{dt}\left(\sum_j X_{ij} A_j^{(i)}\right) = \sum_j A_j^{(i)} \frac{dX_{ij}}{dt}
\end{equation}

\textbf{This is the core equation.}

\begin{itemize}
\item If capacity can move $\rightarrow$ outcomes improve
\item If capacity is frozen $\rightarrow$ misalignment persists
\item If amplification is opaque $\rightarrow$ correction slows
\item If relations are open $\rightarrow$ velocity increases
\end{itemize}

\section*{Plain-Language Translation}

\textbf{Correction velocity measures how quickly capacity flows toward relations that amplify it. Alignment is not a state but a rate: the speed at which misallocation is discovered and reversed. Crucially, amplification is relative: a system only works if it serves the specific goals ($W_i$) of those who maintain it.}
