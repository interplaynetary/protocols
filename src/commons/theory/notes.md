## Communal Production Principles

labor must be general from outset (not particular)

**→ distribution of products = f(communal character of labor)**

The communal character of production would make the product into a communal, general product from the outset. The exchange which originally takes place in production – which would not be an exchange of exchange values but of activities, determined by communal needs and communal purposes – would from the outset include the participation of the individual in the communal world of products.

**→ product character: communal and general (from outset)**
**→ exchange in production = exchange of activities (not exchange values)**
**→ activities determined by: communal needs + communal purposes**
**→ participation in communal products = f(exchange of activities)**

labor is general BEFORE exchange

a communal production, communality, is presupposed as the basis of production. The labour of the individual is posited from the outset as social labour.

**→ mediation: presupposed communality**
**→ individual labor = social labor (from outset)**

Thus, whatever the particular material form of the product he creates or helps to create, what he has bought with his labour is not a specific and particular product, but rather a specific share of the communal production.

**→ labor buys: share of communal production (NOT specific product)**
**→ individual's share of communal production = f(individual's labor contribution)**
**→ share = individual's labor / total social labor**

He therefore has no particular product to exchange. His product is not an exchange value. The product does not first have to be transposed into a particular form in order to attain a general character for the individual. there would take place an organization of labor whose consequence would be the participation of the individual in communal consumption.

**→ product ≠ exchange value (no transposition needed)**
**→ organization of labor → participation in communal consumption**
**→ individual's consumption share = f(organization of labor)**

the social character of production is presupposed, and participation in the world of products, in consumption, is not mediated by the exchange of mutually independent labours or products of labour. It is mediated, rather, by the social conditions of production within which the individual is active.

**→ social character PRESUPPOSED (before production)**

In Communism, the social character of production is presupposed. This presupposition, the world of products, and consumption, are not mediated by exchange (either of mutually independent laborers or producers of labor), but instead by the social conditions of production wherein the individual is active

## Communal Production: Exchange of Activities

The communal character of production makes the products of society communal and general automatically. There ceases exchange of values in production, and instead there is exchange of activities.

**→ communal production → products automatically communal and general**
**→ exchange of values REPLACED BY exchange of activities**

These activities are determined by communal needs, and brings individuals in the communal world of products. The participation of individuals is not mediated by exchange of products, but instead from the independent production of individuals and through presupposition of production (in this case, that production is communal with the labor of individuals automatically assumed as social labor and thus all products being a share of the communal production)

**→ activities = f(communal needs)**
**→ participation mediated by: social conditions of production (NOT product exchange)**
**→ all products = share of communal production**

Thus, no one has any particular product to exchange, and it never takes on such a value form. Rather than there being a division of labor, instead there is an organization of labor wherein individuals participate (under the presumption of social labor) in communal consumption

**→ product never becomes exchange value**
**→ organization of labor → communal consumption**

## Economy of Time: The First Economic Law

On the basis of communal production, the determination of time remains, of course, essential. The less time the society requires to produce wheat, cattle etc., the more time it wins for other production, material or mental.

**→ time for other production = total time - time for necessities**
**→ time saved = reduction in necessary production time**
**→ wealth ∝ (total time - necessary production time)**
**→ share of time for development = (total time - necessary time) / total time**

Just as in the case of an individual, the multiplicity of its development, its enjoyment and its activity depends on economization of time.

**→ development + enjoyment + activity = f(economization of time)**

Economy of time, to this all economy ultimately reduces itself.

**→ ALL ECONOMY = economy of time**

Society likewise has to distribute its time in a purposeful way, in order to achieve a production adequate to its overall needs; just as the individual has to distribute his time correctly in order to achieve knowledge in proper proportions or in order to satisfy the various demands on his activity.

**→ Society must distribute its time in a purposeful way to achieve a production adequate to its overall needs**
**→ Society/Individual must distribute its time correctly in order to achieve knowledge in proper proportions or in order to satisfy the various demands on its activity**
**→ Σ(time allocated to branch i) = total social time**
**→ allocation must satisfy: production ≥ needs**
**→ proportion of time to branch i = time allocated to branch i / total social time**
**→ share of branch i in total time = time_i / Σ(all time)**

Thus, economy of time, along with the planned distribution of labour time among the various branches of production, remains the first economic law on the basis of communal production.

**→ FIRST ECONOMIC LAW = economy of time + planned distribution of labor time among various branches of production**

### The Planning Problem

The stock-book records:
- Products and operations to produce them
- Historical runs: definite quantities produced + labor-time expended (by specific type)
- Current stock and needs

From historical data, we compute **average labor-time per unit** for each operation:
```
average labor-time = Σ(total labor-time) / Σ(quantity produced)
```

**The planning goal:**
- Satisfy needs: produce enough of each product
- Balance labor-time: allocate available labor-time (by specific type) across operations
- Maximize free time: minimize total necessary labor-time

**Material balance (for each product i):**
```
produced_i ≥ final_need_i + intermediate_need_i + reproduction_need_i
```

**Labor-time balance (for each labor type τ):**
```
available_τ ≥ required_τ
```

**Wealth = free time:**
```
T_free = T_total - T_necessary
```

Where `T_necessary` = total labor-time required to satisfy all needs.

**→ The first economic law = conscious distribution of specific kinds of labor-time to satisfy needs while maximizing free time**
**→ Stock-book provides transparent data: what products exist, how to produce them, what labor-time they cost**

labor time in communism = measure of contribution and planning

## Social Labor: Directly Associated Labor

For an example of labour in common or directly associated labour, we have one close at hand in the patriarchal industries of a peasant family, that produces corn, cattle, yarn, linen, and clothing for home use. These different articles are, as regards the society, so many products of its labour, but as between themselves, they are not commodities.

**→ between themselves: social products ≠ commodities (no exchange value)**

The different kinds of labour, such as tillage, cattle tending, spinning, weaving and making clothes, which result in the various products, are in themselves, and such as they are, direct social functions, because functions of the society possesses a spontaneously developed system of division of labour.

**→ different kinds of labor = direct social functions (immediately social) with spontaneously developed system of division of labor**

The distribution of the work within the society, and the regulation of the labour time of the several members, depend as well upon differences of age and sex as upon natural conditions varying with the seasons.

**→ distribution of work & regulation of labor time per member = f(age, sex, natural conditions etc.)**

The labour power of each individual, by its very nature, operates in this case merely as a definite portion of the whole labour power of the society, and therefore, the measure of the expenditure of individual labour power by its duration, appears here by its very nature as a social character of their labour.

**→ individual labor power = portion of total social labor power**
**→ individual's share of social labor = individual labor / total social labor**
**→ portion = individual labor time / Σ(social labor time)**
**→ duration = measure of expenditure of labor power (naturally social)**

## Community of Free Individuals

Let us now picture to ourselves, by way of change, a community of free individuals, carrying on their work with the means of production in common, in which the labour power of all the different individuals is consciously applied as the combined labour power of the community.

**→ total community labor power = Σ(individual labor power) applied consciously**

All the characteristics of Robinson's labour are here repeated, but with this difference, that they are social, instead of individual. Everything produced by him was exclusively the result of his own personal labour, and therefore simply an object of use for himself. The total product of our community is a social product.

**→ total product = social product (not individual)**

One portion serves as fresh means of production and remains social. But another portion is consumed by the members as means of subsistence (individual consumption pool). A distribution of this portion amongst them is consequently necessary.

**→ total product = means of production + individual consumption pool**
**→ individual consumption pool must be distributed among members**

The mode of this distribution will vary with the productive organisation of the community, and the degree of historical development attained by the producers. We will assume, but merely for the sake of a parallel with the production of commodities, that the share of each individual producer in the individual consumption pool is determined by his labour time.

## MODE OF DISTRIBUTION OF INDIVIDUAL CONSUMPTION POOL (VARIES)

**→ mode of distribution = varies based on f(productive organization, historical development attained by producers)**

#### ONLY FOR SAKE OF PARALLEL WITH THE PRODUCTION OF COMMODITIES

ONLY for sake of parallel with the production of commodities:
**→ individual's share of individual consumption pool = f(individual's labor time)**
**→ individual consumption pool share_i = (labor time_i / Σ(labor time)) × total individual consumption pool**
**→ share_i ∝ labor time_i**

Labour time would, in that case, play a double part. Its apportionment in accordance with a definite social plan maintains the proper proportion between the different kinds of work to be done and the various wants of the community.

**→ FIRST ROLE: labor time apportionment in accordance with a definite social plan maintains proper proportion**
**→ proper proportion between different kinds of work to be done and the various wants of community**
**→ apportionment ensures: Σ(labor time_i) needed to achieve production adequate to satisfy Σ(want_i)**

On the other hand, it also serves as a measure of the portion of the common labour borne by each individual, and of his share in the part of the total product destined for individual consumption.

**→ SECOND ROLE: labor time measures contribution**
**→ individual's portion of common labor = individual labor time / total labor time**
**→ individual's share of individual consumption pool ∝ individual's labor time contribution**
**→ share of individual consumption pool_i = (labor time_i / Σ(labor time)) × total individual consumption pool**

####

The social relations of the individual producers, with regard both to their labour and to its products, are in this case perfectly simple and intelligible, and that with regard not only to production but also to distribution.

**→ social relations = simple and intelligible (transparent)**
