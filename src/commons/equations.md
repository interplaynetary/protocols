# Equations and Formulas: Communal Production Economics

## 1. Foundational Premise: Social Character of Production

### The Starting Point
```
→ social character PRESUPPOSED (before production)
→ mediation: presupposed communality
→ individual labor = social labor (from outset)
```

### What This Means for Products
```
→ product character: communal and general (from outset)
→ communal production → products automatically communal and general
→ product ≠ exchange value (no transposition needed)
→ product never becomes exchange value
→ between themselves: social products ≠ commodities (no exchange value)
→ producers do NOT exchange their products
```

### What This Means for Exchange
```
→ exchange of values REPLACED BY exchange of activities
→ exchange in production = exchange of activities (not exchange values)
→ activities determined by: communal needs + communal purposes
→ activities = f(communal needs)
→ participation mediated by: social conditions of production (NOT product exchange)
```

## 2. Total Community Production and Its Decomposition

### Combined Labor Power
```
→ total community labor power = Σ(individual labor power) applied consciously
```

### Total Social Product
```
→ co-operative proceeds of labor = total social product
→ total social product = Σ(all products of labor)
→ total product = social product (not individual)
```

### Decomposition: Production-Related Deductions
```
→ deduction 1: replacement of means of production used up
→ deduction 2: additional portion for expansion of production
→ deduction 3: reserve/insurance funds (accidents, natural calamities)
→ magnitude of deductions = f(available means and forces, computation of probabilities)
→ deductions are NOT calculable by equity (economic necessity)
```

### Remainder: Means of Consumption
```
→ remainder after production deductions = total product - (replacement + expansion + reserves)
→ remainder = means of consumption (intended to serve as means of consumption)
```

### Social/Administrative Deductions
```
→ deduction 4: general costs of administration (not belonging to production)
→ administration costs: very considerably restricted vs present-day society
→ administration costs diminish ∝ new society development
→ Δ(administration costs) ∝ -Δ(society development)

→ deduction 5: common satisfaction of needs (schools, health services, etc.)
→ common needs provision: grows considerably vs present-day society
→ common needs provision grows ∝ new society development
→ Δ(common needs provision) ∝ Δ(society development)

→ deduction 6: funds for those unable to work (poor relief)
```

### Final Structure
```
→ total product = means of production + means of consumption
→ distribution pool = remainder - (administration + common needs + unable to work funds)
→ distribution pool = part of means of consumption divided among individual producers
→ means of consumption must be distributed among members
```

## 3. Economy of Time: The First Economic Law

### The Fundamental Principle
```
→ ALL ECONOMY = economy of time
```

### Time and Wealth
```
→ wealth ∝ (total time - necessary production time)
→ time for other production = total time - time for necessities
→ time saved = reduction in necessary production time
→ share of time for development = (total time - necessary time) / total time
→ wealth = f(reduction in necessary labor time)
→ Δwealth ∝ -Δ(necessary labor time)
→ more wealth ↔ less necessary labor time
```

### Free Time Formula
```
T_free = T_total - T_necessary
```
Where `T_necessary` = total labor-time required to satisfy all needs.

### What Free Time Enables
```
→ development + enjoyment + activity = f(economization of time)
```

## 8. Distributing Time Across Branches of Production

### The Necessity of Time Distribution
```
→ necessity compels: accurate apportionment of time
→ time apportionment = allocation across different kinds of work
→ Society must distribute its time in a purposeful way to achieve a production adequate to its overall needs
→ Society/Individual must distribute its time correctly in order to achieve knowledge in proper proportions or in order to satisfy the various demands on its activity
```

### Time Allocation Constraints
```
→ Σ(time allocated to branch i) = total social time
→ Σ(time for work type i) = total available time for work type i
→ allocation must satisfy: production ≥ needs
```

### Proportional Allocation Formulas
```
→ proportion of time to branch i = time allocated to branch i / total social time
→ share of branch i in total time = time_i / Σ(all time)
→ Σ(labor time in branch i) distributed among the various branches of production to produce enough to satisfy Σ(needs)
→ share of labor to branch i = time_i / Σ(time_j for all branches j)
→ time_i = f(difficulty_i, importance_i, total_available_time)
```

### The First Economic Law (Complete Statement)
```
→ FIRST ECONOMIC LAW = economy of time + planned distribution of labor time among various branches of production
→ The first economic law = conscious distribution of specific kinds of labor-time to satisfy needs while maximizing free time
```

## 9. Stock-Book Accounting: Making It Transparent

### What the Stock-Book Records
```
→ stock-book records: objects of utility + operations necessary for their production + average labor time
→ average labor time for definite quantities = Σ(labor time for definite quantities) / number of labor-times being averaged
→ Stock-book provides transparent data: what products exist, how to produce them, what labor-time they on average cost us
```

### Average Labor-Time Calculation
```
average labor-time = Σ(total labor-time) / Σ(quantity produced)
```

### The Result: Transparency
```
→ relations: social labor ↔ objects of wealth (simple and transparent)
→ no mystification, no commodity fetishism
→ social relations = simple and intelligible (transparent)
```

### Social Relations
```
→ social relations of production = personal dependence (direct, not mediated by commodities)
→ production relations = same character as other life spheres
```

## 10. The Planning Problem: Mathematical Formulation

### Planning Goals
- Satisfy needs: produce enough of each product
- Balance labor-time: allocate available labor-time (by specific type) across operations
- Maximize free time: minimize total necessary labor-time

### Material Balance Constraint (for each product i)
```
produced_i ≥ final_need_i + intermediate_need_i + reproduction_need_i
```

### Labor-Time Balance Constraint (for each labor type τ)
```
available_τ ≥ required_τ
```

### Objective: Maximize Free Time
```
maximize: T_free = T_total - T_necessary
```

## 3. Individual Labor and Shares

### Individual Labor as Part of the Whole
```
→ individual labor exists directly as component part of total labor
→ individual labor power = portion of total social labor power
→ individual's share of social labor = individual labor / total social labor
→ portion = individual labor time / Σ(social labor time)
→ duration = measure of expenditure of labor power (naturally social)
```

### What Individual Labor Buys
```
→ labor buys: share of communal production (NOT specific product)
→ individual's share of communal production = f(individual's labor contribution)
→ share = individual's labor / total social labor
→ all products = share of communal production
```

### Participation in Consumption
```
→ organization of labor = communal consumption
→ individual's consumption share = f(organization of labor)
```

### Comparing Labor Across Branches
```
→ comparison across branches: use average labor time
→ average labor time for type τ = Σ(labor time in τ) / count(workers in τ)
```

### Factors Affecting Labor Time Distribution
```
→ distribution of work & regulation of labor time per member = f(age, sex, natural conditions etc.)
```

## 12. Distribution as Consequence of Production Relations

### Fundamental Relation
```
→ distribution of means of consumption = consequence of distribution of conditions of production
→ distribution of conditions of production = feature of mode of production itself
→ mode of production determines → distribution of production conditions → distribution of consumption
```

### Capitalist Mode
```
→ material conditions of production in hands of nonworkers (capital + land property)
→ masses own only: personal condition of production (labor power)
→ if production elements so distributed → present-day consumption distribution results automatically
```

### Co-operative Mode
```
→ if material conditions of production = co-operative property of workers
→ then: distribution of means of consumption different from present one results
→ co-operative ownership of production conditions → different consumption distribution
```

### Hypothetical: For Sake of Parallel with Commodity Production
```
→ individual's share of individual consumption pool = f(individual's labor time)
→ means of consumption share_i = (labor time_i / Σ(labor time)) × total means of consumption
→ share_i ∝ labor time_i
→ share of individual consumption pool_i = (labor time_i / Σ(labor time)) × total means of consumption
```

### Labor Time: The Dual Role

#### First Role: Planning and Apportionment
```
→ FIRST ROLE: labor time apportionment in accordance with a definite social plan maintains proper proportion
→ proper proportion between different kinds of work to be done and the various wants of community
→ apportionment ensures: Σ(labor time_i) needed to achieve production adequate to satisfy Σ(want_i)
```

#### Second Role: Measuring Contribution
```
→ SECOND ROLE: labor time measures contribution
→ individual's portion of common labor = individual labor time / total labor time
→ individual's share of individual consumption pool ∝ individual's labor time contribution
```

## 4. Labor Certificates and Equal Exchange (First Phase)

### The Exchange Principle
```
→ individual producer receives back from society = exactly what individual gives to society (after deductions)
→ what individual gives = individual quantum of labor
→ social working day = Σ(individual hours of work)
→ individual labor time = part of social working day contributed by individual
→ individual's share in social working day = individual labor time / social working day
```

### Labor Certificates Mechanism
```
→ individual receives certificate: furnished X amount of labor (after deducting labor for common funds)
→ with certificate: draws from social stock of individual consumption pool
→ consumption drawn = amount equal to labor cost
→ same amount of labor given in one form = received back in another form
```

### Principle of Equal Values
```
→ same principle as exchange of commodities (exchange of equal values)
→ content and form changed (altered circumstances)
→ no one can give anything except labor
→ nothing can pass to ownership of individuals except individual means of consumption
→ given amount of labor in one form = exchanged for equal amount of labor in another form
```

### Equal Right = Bourgeois Right (in First Phase)
```
→ equal right in principle = bourgeois right
→ principle and practice no longer at loggerheads
→ exchange of equivalents exists in individual case (not just on average)
```

## 5. Measurement of Labor: Standards and Inequality

### The Equal Standard
```
→ right of producers ∝ labor they supply
→ equality consists in: measurement made with equal standard (labor)
→ labor as measure must be defined by: duration OR intensity
→ without duration/intensity definition: labor ceases to be standard of measurement
```

### Qualitative vs Quantitative Differences
```
→ labor differs: quantitatively AND qualitatively
→ different kinds of labor = direct social functions (immediately social) with spontaneously developed system of division of labor
→ quantitative comparison requires: qualitative equivalence
→ measuring labor quantitatively assumes: the identity of their quality
```

### Unequal Individuals, Equal Standard
```
→ equal right = unequal right for unequal labor
→ recognizes no class differences (everyone is only a worker)
→ tacitly recognizes: unequal individual endowment + productive capacity as natural privilege
→ right = right of inequality (in its content)
→ right consists in: application of equal standard
→ unequal individuals measurable by equal standard only when: brought under equal point of view
→ individuals regarded only as workers (everything else ignored)
```

### Inequality in Outcomes
```
→ equal performance of labor + equal share in social consumption fund → unequal actual receipt
→ one worker married, another not; one has more children, etc.
→ with equal labor performance: one receives more than another, one richer than another
→ to avoid defects: right would have to be unequal (instead of equal)
```

### Constraints of First Phase
```
→ defects inevitable in first phase of communist society
→ right can never be higher than: economic structure of society + cultural development conditioned thereby
→ right ≤ f(economic structure, cultural development)
```
